Hi, I'm Mason. I graduated from UW–Madison in 2025 with a B.S. in Data Science,
plus certificates in Computer Science and Economics. Right now I'm looking for
roles in data analytics, business analytics, or quant work. I like turning
messy data into discoveries and visualizations.

Most of what's on this site came from following a question further than I
probably needed to. A class project on statistical analysis and regressions
on market data got me into financial analytics. Afterward, I followed my
curiosity, which led me to brainstorming, building, testing, and deploying my
own trading strategy. I then pressed onward toward current affairs and
decided I wanted to construct a visualizer for modern political trading,
since it's become a hot topic in recent times. I hoped to make something
people of my generation could comprehend, and built an ELO system like that
of chess or video games, so that someone my age could understand who might be
using their insider knowledge of the markets to their advantage.

## What I work with

- **Languages:** Python, R, SQL, Java
- **Tools:** Google Cloud, Docker, Git
- **Areas:** data analysis, statistical modeling, machine learning, backtesting

## Projects

- **[Overnight Gaps and the 30-Minute ORB](/research/gap-orb-momentum-continuation/)**:
  a study of whether leveraged ETFs keep running in the direction of a big
  overnight gaps.
- **[Fama–French Factor Analysis](/projects/fama-french-factor-analysis/)**: a
  class project using multiple-testing corrections and cross-validated LASSO
  to test whether standard equity risk factors predict next-month returns.
- **[Congressional Trading ELO](/projects/congressional-trading-elo/)**: a
  self-updating leaderboard that scores members of Congress' disclosed stock
  trades as head-to-head matches against the S&P 500, built with Python and
  GitHub Actions.
