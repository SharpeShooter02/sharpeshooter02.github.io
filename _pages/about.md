Hi, I'm Mason. I graduated from the University of Wisconsin–Madison in 2025 with
a B.S. in Data Science (plus certificates in Computer Science and Economics), and
I'm looking for roles in data analytics, business analytics, or quantitative
analysis. I like taking messy, real-world data and turning it into something
clear and useful — and I care about doing it carefully.

Most of what's on this site started as me following my curiosity a little too
far. I got interested in markets, so I built a backtesting setup to check whether
a trading idea actually held up once you accounted for costs and stopped fooling
yourself with look-ahead bias. Then, for a class project, I dug into whether the
famous Fama–French factors actually predict anything month to month (short
answer: not really). I'm not a Wall Street quant — I'm a new grad who finds this
stuff genuinely fun and tries to be honest about what the data does and doesn't
show.

## What I work with

- **Languages:** Python, R, SQL, Java
- **Tools:** Google Cloud, Docker, Git
- **Areas:** data analysis, statistical modeling, machine learning, backtesting

## Projects

- **[Overnight Gaps and the 30-Minute ORB](/research/gap-orb-mean-reversion/)** —
  a backtest of an intraday mean-reversion idea on leveraged ETFs, with realistic
  transaction costs and a focus on not tricking myself with look-ahead bias.
- **[Fama–French Factor Analysis](/projects/fama-french-factor-analysis/)** — a
  class project using multiple-testing corrections and cross-validated LASSO to
  ask whether standard equity risk factors predict next-month returns.
- **[Congressional Trading ELO](/projects/congressional-trading-elo/)** — a
  self-updating leaderboard that scores members of Congress' disclosed stock
  trades as head-to-head matches against the S&P 500, built with Python and
  GitHub Actions.
